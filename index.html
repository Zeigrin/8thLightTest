Celebrate Paul Bunyon days!

Paul Bunyan is a lumberjack character in North American and Michigan folklore. One of the most famous and popularfolklore heroes, he is usually described as a giant as well as a lumberjack possessed of amazing skills. In most tales, he is accompanied Babe, his famous blue ox. The State of Michigan has designated Oscoda as the official home of Paul Bunyan. Oscoda, Michigan celebrates in September with the Paul Bunyan Days Festival. Along with lots of food, drink and carnival rides, the festival includes Games, BBQ Competition, a Classic Car Show, Motorcycle Show, Chainsaw Carving Competition, Paul Bunyan Look-alike Contest, Pie Throwing, Musical Entertainment, Ax Throwing, Log Chopping and free kids games.

The Ausable River Canoe Marathon

he AuSable River Canoe Marathon, a.k.a. "The Marathon", is a non-stop canoe race that starts at night with a thrilling LeMans-style running-start to the river in Grayling, MI, and ends 120 miles later near the shores of Lake Huron in Oscoda, MI. Contestants must navigate the narrow, winding upper stretch in total darkness, as well as stump-filled ponds and the blazing July sun in the lower stretch. This race is not a recreational canoe float, but a professional, ultra-competitive race with the very best professional paddlers from around North and Central America. Contestants must be in peak condition in order to complete the race in the allotted time, and to endure the grueling and strenuous physical and mental strains The Marathon demands of the paddlers.

The shores of Lake Huron

Oscoda Beach Park has approximately one-thousand and twenty-five feet (1,025) of Lake Huron frontage and includes a skateboard park, a small basketball court, a band shell, a pavilion, children’s play equipment, picnic tables and grills, benches, a bathhouse with restrooms and a paved parking area and it’s all handicapped accessible. The bathhouse is surrounded by a sunbathing and observation deck with a lighted boardwalk leading to the south along the shoreline and linking to a street-scape pathway originating in the central business district.
